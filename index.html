Design Principle

Crime must be the more attractive path. A civilian grinding legal jobs at $100k clean/hr should look at criminals earning $200k dirty/hr and feel the pull. Even after laundering at a 30% cut, a solo criminal takes home $140k clean/hr — 40% more than any legal job. That gap is the hook that turns civilians into criminals.

Gang Economy

Gangs take a 20% cut of their members' dirty earnings. This funds the gang treasury for territory, equipment, and paying members who aren't on active jobs. From their 20% intake, gang leaders can afford to pay soldiers ~50% back as wages/stipends. A gang member doing crime still clears $112k clean/hr after both the gang cut and laundering — still 12% above legal work.

Drug Economy Analysis

Weed is the entry drug. Full-cycle clean rate is below legal ($57k vs $100k), but the $125k/hr dirty sell rate still beats legal for anyone spending within the illegal economy. It's the gateway.

Coke hits the $200k dirty/hr target during active selling. Full-cycle clean rate ($92k) nearly matches legal. During active selling, the clean rate ($140k) clearly beats it.

Meth is the premium drug. Full-cycle effective rate exceeds legal jobs by 39% even in clean money. During active selling, it's $210k clean — more than double legal.

Fragments from primary and secondary sources are the same item — they stack. A player could get 2 fragments from Pacific Standard and 1 from Casino Penthouse to complete the Armour Mould.

Blueprint Design Rules

  1. Fragments are personal. Cannot be traded, sold, or dropped.
  2. Crafted items are NOT permanent. Weapons degrade and armour breaks. Creates ongoing material sink.
  3. Crafted items CAN be lost. If you die and get looted, or get arrested, the item is gone.
  4. Re-crafting is cheaper. Once complete, future crafts only need materials + money (no fragments).
  5. Fragments persist through death/arrest. Stored in separate blueprint inventory that isn't lootable.

Key Balance Points

  1. Crime always out-earns legal work — at every level. Even weed at $81k dirty/hr beats legal when spent in the illegal economy. Chain finals like Main Cayo ($620k dirty/hr) are life-changing paydays.
  2. Gang members still out-earn legal workers. After the 20% gang cut AND 30% laundering, a gang member running meth still clears $111k clean/hr. No scenario where a criminal is worse off than a legal grinder.
  3. Gangs can fund themselves. A gang with 4 active members doing a Cayo chain generates ~$592k for the treasury per run.
  4. Heists are weekly events, not daily grinds. A full Cayo chain pays $740k per player — enough to fund a week of operations.
  5. Six chains create variety and specialisation. Banking for cash. Luxury for items. Cayo for jackpots. Casino for quick runs. Rural for crafting. Military for weapons.
  6. Blueprint fragments create long-term chase. The endgame loop — weeks of grinding toward a Gold AK or Heavy Tactical Armour.
